The Firm

Selling to the wrong buyer can undo decades of deliberate work in a matter of months. When a buyer treats your company like a short-term asset, the culture gets dismantled, people get cut, and local ties get severed by ownership with no roots in the community, all in the hope that they can flip the business in a few years and make a quick buck.

Southland Equity was founded by Cameron Baird to acquire and steward exceptional Georgia businesses long-term. We operate what we buy and stay accountable to every commitment because the person who shakes your hand at the start is the owner when the deal closes.

Why Southland

What makes us different.

Real Business Roots
You might worry about selling to an MBA who tries to run your company from a textbook. We are operators, not institutional financiers. Having scaled businesses from scratch, we look at your operation through real-world experience, not financial theories.
Hands-On Support
Many owners fear that unbuttoned records will kill a deal. A business sale requires gathering massive amounts of historical data. If your files aren’t perfectly organized, don’t worry — we are ready to chip in and help.
Georgia by Choice
It is natural to fear an out-of-state buyer who will never meet your team or care about your community. We focus on Georgia by design. True stewardship requires actually belonging to the local community you serve.
The Process

What to expect.

A typical transaction takes four to six months from start to finish. The process has natural peaks and valleys, and it can feel overwhelming at times. We’ve been there before. We stay locked in with you the entire way, solving problems together until the deal is funded.

01
A Confidential Conversation
No paperwork, no obligations. We want to understand what you built and what a good outcome looks like for you personally.
02
Offer & Diligence
We issue a formal offer (Letter of Intent) within a few weeks of reviewing your core records. We then take 60 to 90 days to verify the data, clear bank underwriting, negotiate the purchase agreement, and fund the sale.
03
Close & Transition
You get paid. We assume the responsibility. Your employees stay, your customers feel no disruption, and every commitment we made is honored.
